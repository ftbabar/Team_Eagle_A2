Group Processes

Team Eagle worked very well together for Assignment 2. Every member was active and contributed well throughout the assignment. Tasks were divided evenly, and members were always ready to voluntarily help others when needed. Everyone appreciated each other’s efforts. In terms of delivery of work, all members ensured their tasks were completed on time and hence, the team managed to submit work that they were satisfied with. Observing our work in assignment 2, we now have a better understanding of each members capabilities and strengths which have been considered when dividing our tasks for assignment 3. Although the team faced no challenges in communication, there were some problems due to our overall lack of experience with MS Teams and GitHub. Learning from our work in assignment 2, the group has made some changes in the way we use MS Teams. To avoid confusion, a new channel was created for A3 communication purposes. A folder was created for all meeting agenda/minutes files. This will allow members to easily locate them when working on the tools section. The team will follow a uniform format when noting down meeting agendas and minutes. We will be also creating new folders to categorise all our files as we go along. This was something we did later on in assignment 2. Managing and keeping our branches up to date with each other was a major problem that we faced throughout assignment 2. In order to avoid wasting time in merging our previous branches, we have deleted all our individual branches and created new ones from the master branch. Each member will make sure to pull in all changes from the master branch to their personal branches whenever they plan on making some changes to the HTML or CSS.

Comparison between the team members’ ideal jobs and career paths

A major part of work in the IT field consists of constant learning and hence all members of the team plan to complete micro courses and university level subjects/degrees as a part of their career plans. This is normally expected within the IT field as new technologies are constantly being introduced which change the way we work.

Both Hannah and Fatima plan on working in the design field, but the skills required for their ideal jobs and career plans are very different. This is mainly because of the experience both members have. While one is working towards a senior digital designer role, the other is already a digital designer and hopes to become a Research design director. However, there is still some similarity in their career plans as both aim to work as a UX/UI designer to gain the experience and skills required for their desired roles.

On the other hand, although Sarah Carney’s and Hannah’s ideal jobs may sound totally different, there is similarity in the generic and IT skills required for both these roles. Some the common skills include:

  • Team building

  • Relationship management

Despite the similarities of generic/IT skills the two members have very different career plans to achieve their goals (as mentioned above). Of course, this is due to the differing nature of their ideal jobs.

The skills required for Sarah Brinckman’s ideal job (Mobile developer) have similarities with most of the other members’ job skills. There are also similarities between Sarah Brinckman and Fatima’s career plans apart from their planned studies. While Fatima hopes to Major in Design with elective subjects from Information Technology, Sarah Brinckman plans to do the opposite with the main focus on a Bachelor of Information Technology consisting of a minor in Internet design. Alongside their studies, both aim to work some entry level jobs in their respective areas to gain experience and enhance their portfolios.
